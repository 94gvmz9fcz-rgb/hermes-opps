## I. Branch-Specific Acronyms

### 🇺🇸 U.S. Air Force

| Acronym | Expansion | Branch Scope | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AFNWC** | Air Force Nuclear Weapons Center | USAF (primary) | Nuclear enterprise. Specific mission — not a general IT entry point. |
| **AFLCMC** | Air Force Life Cycle Management Center | USAF (primary), 🚀 Space Force (secondary) | **Josh's key customer entry point.** Manages AF acquisitions across platforms, IT, cyber, and weapons systems. Some Space Force programs also route through AFLCMC. |
| **AFSOC** | Air Force Special Operations Command | USAF | Special ops IT and comms needs. Not a primary Josh target but relevant for specific programs. |

| **AMC** | Air Mobility Command | USAF | Tanker/transport fleet. Logistics IT and fleet modernization. |
| **AFMC** | Air Force Materiel Command | USAF | Parent command to AFLCMC. Lifecycle management of AF systems. |

## II. Joint / Multi-Branch Acronyms

| Acronym | Expansion | Branch Scope | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| **JADC2** | Joint All-Domain Command and Control | **All branches** | Multi-branch initiative. Every branch has their "slice" of JADC2 — AF has ABMS, Army has Project Convergence. Not a single program, a capability vision. |
| **C2** | Command and Control | Universal | Generic. Context determines branch and capability area. |
| **C4ISR** | Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Intelligence, Surveillance & Reconnaissance | Universal | Umbrella term. Very common in DoD IT sales — catch-all for the comms/IT/intel space. |
| **ISR** | Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance | Universal | Specific capability pillar. Often used standalone: "ISR platforms," "ISR modernization." |
| **C5ISR** | Command, Control, Computers, Communications, Cyber, Intelligence, Surveillance & Reconnaissance | Universal (Army-heavy) | Extended C4ISR + cyber. Army uses this more than other branches. |
| **INDOPACOM** | Indo-Pacific Command | Joint (combatant command) | Josh's expansion target. Pacific theater — Hawaii, Guam, Japan, Korea. |

## III. Contract Vehicles & Acquisition

| Acronym | Expansion | Type | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GSA** | General Services Administration | Contract Vehicle | Federal procurement backbone. Multiple schedules. Most federal buyers can use GSA. |
| **SEWP** | Solutions for Enterprise-Wide Procurement | Contract Vehicle (NASA-managed, DoD-accessible) | IT-focused. Very common in Josh's space. NASA SEWP V is the current iteration. |

| **RFP** | Request for Proposal | Acquisition Phase | Formal solicitation. |
| **RFI** | Request for Information | Acquisition Phase | Pre-RFP — market research. |
| **POC** | Proof of Concept | Acquisition Phase | Technical validation step. Also: Point of Contact (context-dependent). |

## VII. Resolution Rules

When I encounter an acronym in your voice, text, or enclosures:

1. **Exact match in registry** → use the branch scope and context to interpret
2. **Known expansion, unknown context** → check for branch clues in surrounding content (e.g., if "AFLCMC" appears next to "Space Force," note the cross-branch usage)
3. **Partial match** (e.g., "PEO" alone) → flag for context resolution — which PEO? Which branch?
4. **No match** → research from public DoD sources, build a 🔵 Confident entry, flag for your review

### Standardization Rule

Every acronym added to the registry gets, at minimum:
- Expansion
- Primary branch/scope
- Context note (what it means in Josh's world, not just the government definition)

**No acronym left unresolved in a session.** Any new acronym encountered during a conversation gets an entry before the conversation ends.
