## The Hunter's Framework

### Three Motions

| Motion | What It Is | Josh's Current State |
|---|---|---|
| **Prospecting** | Identifying and qualifying new opportunities | Active — primary focus |
| **Landing** | Winning the first deal with a new customer | Current phase — building pipeline to first close |
| **Expanding** | Growing within an existing account | Future phase — once land is achieved |

### The Land Sequence

```
Conference/Event
    ↓
Initial contact (cold or warm introduction)
    ↓
Qualify: budget, authority, need, timeline
    ↓
Customer site visit / whiteboarding session
    ↓
Technical validation (POC, demo, evaluation)
    ↓
Contract vehicle alignment
    ↓
Proposal / BAFO
    ↓
Win → Land
    ↓
Execute → Build credibility → Expand
```

## Plays & Patterns (Initial)

*These are observations so far. They'll sharpen with more data.*

### Play: Conference-to-Pipeline

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Pre-event: identify target accounts and personas attending |
| 2 | At event: quality interactions over quantity — 5-8 strong conversations > 30 business cards |
| 3 | Capture: voice note to Herms or note in CRM same day |
| 4 | Follow-up: within 24-48 hours with something of value |
| 5 | Bridge: schedule a site visit or whiteboarding session within 2 weeks |
| 6 | Pipeline: move to active opportunity |

### Play: Cold Start (No Relationships)

When you have zero incumbent advantage:
1. **Find the pain** — new program, failed initiative, budget mandate, compliance deadline
2. **Find the champion** — someone with a problem so painful they'll take a call from a stranger
3. **Find the door** — contract vehicle, SI partner, previous coworker, conference connection
4. **Bring value first** — intel, market map, competitive analysis, relevant past win story
5. **Earn the meeting** — you don't ask for it until you've given something

### Play: Whiteboarding Session

Heavy emphasis in Josh's motion. Best practices for a hunter:
- **Set the agenda before you walk in:** "I want to understand your [priority] and map it against [solution category]"
- **Listen 80%, talk 20%** — whiteboarding is about drawing *their* problem, not your solution
- **Leave behind:** a 1-page PDF that summarizes the conversation and gives them something to champion internally
- **Follow-up:** within 48 hours, send the whiteboard notes + 3 specific next steps
