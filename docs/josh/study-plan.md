## How This Works

I study *while* you work. Every week I pick a focus area, research it, and deliver a **brief** that makes you sharper by Friday. You don't study — I do. You absorb.

| Day | What I Do | What You Get |

## Weeks 1-4: Foundation

### Week 1: Your Specific Technology Landscape
*Research what you sell, who buys it, and why they buy it from you vs. competitors*
- Refresh: the specific tech categories you mentioned earlier
- Market map: major OEMs, SI partners, competitors in your space
- Buyer personas: who actually signs the PO at your target accounts
- **Deliverable:** Filled-in technology section of industry-landscape.md

### Week 2: Your Territory — Western US DoD
*Deep map of your accounts by base, command, and program office*
- Air Force bases in your territory: who's who, what programs are active
- Space Force: how they buy differently from the other branches
- Army (West + Madison): command structure at Redstone, key PEOs
- **Deliverable:** Account-by-account cheat sheet

### Week 3: The Huntsville / Redstone Ecosystem
*If Madison is a key node, we own this territory*
- Major players: SIs, primes, OEMs with ARAs there
- Contract vehicles active on Redstone
- Upcoming RFPs, industry days, and networking events
- **Deliverable:** Huntsville field guide + event calendar

### Week 4: INDOPACOM — The Expansion Target
*Early groundwork for when you're ready*
- INDOPACOM structure: where they buy, what they buy, who decides
- Key bases: Hawaii (Camp Smith, Pearl), Guam (Andersen), Japan (Yokota, Kadena)
- The logistics of selling into PACOM from the mainland
- **Deliverable:** INDOPACOM entry strategy memo

## Weeks 5-8: Relationship & Pipeline Science

### Week 5: The Conference Playbook
*Maximize ROI from every event*
- Pre-event targeting framework
- The "conversation to pipeline" conversion play
- Post-event follow-up sequence that actually gets meetings
- **Deliverable:** Conference SOP you can run in your sleep

### Week 6: Federal Contract Vehicles Deep Dive
*Know which vehicles matter, how to get on them, and how to use them*
- GSA, SEWP, IT-70, and branch-specific vehicles
- How prime contractors and SIs use them (and how you work with them)
- Getting on the right schedule as a new rep
- **Deliverable:** Contract vehicle roadmap

### Week 7: The SI Partner Playbook
*Your biggest leverage point is the people who already have the contract*
- Top SIs in your territory: who to partner with, who to watch
- The sub-kick / teaming agreement play
- How to make yourself valuable to a prime (so they bring you in)
- **Deliverable:** SI partner target list + outreach templates

### Week 8: Competitive Intel Framework
*Know what you're walking into before every call*
- How to map the competitive landscape in a new account
- Incumbent displacement strategy (when you can and can't)
- Win/loss analysis framework (I build this as deals come in)
- **Deliverable:** Competitive battle card template

## Weeks 9-12: Strategic Execution

### Week 9: The First Deal — Landing Playbook
*From prospect to first PO — the complete sequence*
- Pipeline stages specific to DoD sales
- The "six contacts per account" rule
- How to navigate a long sales cycle without losing momentum
- **Deliverable:** Personalized deal progression tracker

### Week 10: Price to Win & Positioning
*How the deal gets priced, positioned, and defended*
- DoD pricing dynamics (LPTA vs. best value)

- Value messaging: why your solution beats the incumbent

### Week 11: The Whiteboarding Masterclass
*Turning every whiteboarding session into a land*
- 10 whiteboard frameworks for DoD IT conversations
- Reading the room: when to pivot, when to push, when to close
- The artifact: what to leave behind that gets you the next meeting
- **Deliverable:** Whiteboarding field manual

### Week 12: Review & Reset
*Where are we? What's working? What changed?*
- Win/loss analysis on everything so far
- Pipeline health check
- Territory reassessment
- Study Plan 2.0 based on real-world data
- **Deliverable:** Quarter 1 retrospective + Q2 plan
